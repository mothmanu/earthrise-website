The Journey

Six challenges. One think tank.

The E-Lab took us from introducing ourselves on camera to pitching for a $10,000 grant — and finally to building this website. Here's every step of the road.

1Challenge 01

Introduction — "Get To Know Me"

Who are you? What drives you? We kicked off our E-Lab journey with a team elevator pitch: each member shared their name, country, interests, a fun fact, and what they bring to the team, along with a group statement on what we hoped to gain from E-Lab.

It was the moment five strangers became EarthRise.

1,700 points
2Challenge 02

Discover Africa

There is power in telling our own stories. For this challenge we chose an African community and told a story the world hadn't heard — its amazing facts, misconceptions, problems, and uniqueness — using a creative media format, with every member playing a role.

It sharpened the storytelling, creative, critical, and radical thinking skills we'd lean on for the rest of the journey.

1,800 points
3Challenge 03

Help-Lab

Community engagement means striving to improve the quality of life of a given community — and this was our chance. We chose a cause, added value to it, and documented the work along with our reflections: what we contributed, which skills we leveraged and learned, and the long-term benefits of our act.

Getting our hands dirty for a community brought the waste problem out of the workbook and into real life.

2,000 points
4Challenge 04

Hunt for Treasure

"Knowledge is the best and safest treasure to acquire." For this research challenge, we interviewed Cleaning & Greening Services — a Kigali waste management company operating in Nyamirambo since 2019 — to understand their mission, daily operations, challenges, and future plans.

Their struggles echoed our research: permits are hard to obtain, investors are scarce, and landfills sometimes re-mix waste that's already been sorted. Their plans for electric trucks and the Isuku Pay platform showed us where the industry is heading.

2,500 points
5Challenge 05

Launch Your Mission

The unexpected task: compete for a (hypothetical) $10,000 grant. Drawing on our mission, problem statement, and research, we produced a full project proposal and a three-minute video pitch covering who we are, the problem, our solution, and why we need the money.

Our budget put 35% into the pilot bag batch, 20% into multi-bin systems, 20% into pilot logistics, 15% into REMA licensing, and 10% into community education — with a six-month roadmap from permits to a scale-up plan.

2,000 points
6Challenge 06 · You are here

Digital Print

In the 21st century, digital literacy and presentation skills are essential for anyone seeking to positively impact their continent. The final challenge: create a website showcasing our E-Lab journey — our think tank, the individuals who make it up, and everything we built along the way.

You're looking at it right now. Welcome to EarthRise.

5,000 points
