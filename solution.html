Our Solution

Sorting made simple, compliant, and worth it.

An end-to-end source-segregation ecosystem: color-coded compostable bags for households, multi-bin systems for businesses, and a marketplace that turns sorted organic waste into income.

The products

Two products, zero plastic footprint

Rwanda's first fully compliant, color-coded source-segregation system, using certified biodegradable containment materials that leave no plastic behind during composting or recycling.

🟢 Color-coded compostable bags · for households

Households receive two distinct colors of locally manufactured, 100% compostable waste bags — Green for organic and Blue for inorganic — used indoors before being transferred to centralized community collection points.

  • 100% biodegradable — zero plastic footprint in composting
  • Locally manufactured in Rwanda
  • Sold at 1,000 RWF, with compost income returned to the household

🗂️ Multi-bin systems · for businesses

For high-volume commercial clients — hotels, restaurants, corporations — we deploy heavy-duty, divided multi-bin systems tailored to scale with daily business waste generation, so sorting happens at the source, every day.

  • Divided compartments matched to each business's waste profile
  • Built to commercial volume and durability needs
  • Reduces disposal costs while creating a new revenue stream
How it works

From kitchen scraps to farmer's field

STEP 01

Sort at the source

Households and businesses separate waste into Green (organic) and Blue (inorganic) bags or divided bins.

STEP 02

Collect at the hub

Bags are dropped at centralized community collection points and gathered — sorted waste stays sorted.

STEP 03

Sell to farmers

Organic waste is sold directly to farmers and agricultural firms as compost feedstock, replacing imported fertiliser.

STEP 04

Return the income

Households and businesses earn a share of every sale — sorting finally pays.

Operations begin upon securing a government business permit and a manufacturing license from the Rwanda Environment Management Authority (REMA), with initial capital raised through green enterprise grants and public-private partnerships. A 3-month pilot across selected households and businesses will optimize collection logistics and measure segregation compliance before full launch.

Expected impact · Year one

What success looks like

🏠

500+ households

Enrolled in the pilot programme across selected neighbourhoods of Kigali in the first year.

📉

10% reduction

In organic waste arriving at Nduba Landfill from participating areas.

💰

~1,000 RWF/month

Income per sorting household, based on ~20kg of organic waste per month at the RWF 75/kg market rate.

🌍

Environmental

Less organic waste at Nduba, lower greenhouse gas emissions from decomposing waste, and sustainable waste practices that stick.

📈

Economic

Lower disposal costs for businesses, value created from waste through compost sales, and support for Rwanda's circular economy.

🤝

Social

Stronger partnerships between businesses and farmers, greater awareness of sorting at the source, and new jobs in collection and processing.
